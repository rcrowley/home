  • Katherine’s childhood home

    I grew up in the charming town of Winnetka, a suburb just north of Chicago.  I have such fond memories of our house on tree-lined Oak Street.  My sister Stephanie and I took more than a few walks to the Winnetka Bakery for smiley-face cookies which we had to protect from our brothers, Christopher, Jonathan, and Benjamin. — Katherine

  • The Crowley residence

    I grew up in Danville, Kentucky, a town of 15,000 people that all know each other.  I can think of no better place to grow up and start my journey west.  After a family vacation in 1997 I thought my destination was Seattle and only detoured to San Francisco in 2006.  My sisters Charlotte and Caroline, and I try to make it back here to visit our folks as often as we can. — Richard

  • Hurd Hall

    We met in April of 2005 during my sophomore year at Wash U.  Katherine was the RA I wish I’d met sooner. — Richard

  • Brookings Hall

    Although I was trying to cram for an art history exam on the steps, I was gladly distracted when I bumped into Richard and we stopped to catch up for a while. — Katherine

  • Rigazzi’s

    On our first date, Richard won me over with his charm, his manners, and of course, his choice of restaurant: an Italian place on The Hill with red gingham tablecloths and food that can’t be beat.  I had the spicy arrabiata, he had the baked Italian sausage pasta.  After an eight-hour first date that we didn’t want to end, we were off to a great start. — Katherine

  • First apartment in San Francisco

    After a year and a half apart with many visits between San Francisco and Saint Louis, Katherine was finishing graduate school and moving west.  We spent one of her weekend visits checking out every neighborhood in town, settled on Russian Hill, and found an apartment with a killer view of the Golden Gate Bridge but a tiny kitchen. — Richard

  • Proposal in Aix-en-Provence

    On our first big vacation together, we went for a long hike in the beautiful countryside of Provence.  After getting back to town and finding a charming park for our picnic, I thought Richard was digging around in his bag for some goat cheese and handi-wipes.  Instead, he pulled out something much better! — Katherine

  • Alamo Square

    A great picnic spot and home of the famous Painted Ladies, the six awesome Victorian houses you may recognize from the opening credits of Full House.  Just down the hill from here is Hayes Valley, a great place for dining and shopping.

  • Alta Plaza

    A park with some of the best views of the city and a common stop on our walks past the mansions of Pacific Heights.

  • Amarena

    An Italian restaurant on Russian Hill that’s become our go-to happy hour with delicious (and free) antipasti and great wine on Thursday nights.

  • AT&T Park

    Home of the San Francisco Giants, who are in town playing the Los Angeles Dodgers on the weekend of our wedding.  Even if you don’t go to a game, check out the ballpark, and walk along the bay on the Embarcadero.

    The Giants are at home, playing the Padres, Dodgers, and Mets from Monday, July 23rd through Thursday, August 2nd.  Details and tickets »

  • Bar Jules

    My absolute favorite restaurant with a seasonal menu of the best California has to offer, prepared simply on a wood fire in their open kitchen.  I always pick this place whenever we get an excuse to celebrate. — Katherine

  • Bix

    An upscale restaurant with a jazz vibe, killer cocktails, and terrific food (get the hash). We owe Marti Pollom a big thanks for introducing this place to us.

  • Blazing Saddles

    Though San Francisco is full of hills, a bike ride is a fantastic way to see the city.  This places rents bikes by the hour and provides maps for rides along the bay and over the Golden Gate Bridge, to Sausalito, and beyond.  Or ask us about our favorite route through the city, known affectionately as the Butter Loop.

  • Bullitt

    A neighborhood favorite bar with great burgers and unbelievable tater tots.  Katherine goes for the sweet potato tots.  Richard goes for the classic.

  • City Beer Store

    A beer-lover’s dream.  Part bar, part retail and a selection like no other.

  • Dipsea

    If you find yourself across the bridge, this brunch place serves excellent biscuits and gravy.

  • Ferry Building

    A food-lover’s paradise and home to a farmer’s market on Saturdays and Tuesdays.  Inside there are lots of specialty shops, including a gluten-free bakery.  A great place to begin a walk along the Embarcadero towards AT&T Park for a Giants game.

  • Frascati

    Tough-to-beat neighborhood restaurant serving excellent California cuisine along the Hyde Street cable car.

  • Front Porch

    Fried chicken in a popcorn bucket.

  • House of Nanking

    Arguably the best Chinese food in San Francisco.  Make sure to order plenty of the sesame chicken with sweet potatoes.

  • Just For You

    Katherine’s favorite brunch place.  Purveyor of beignets, biscuits, and gluten-free bread.

  • Lafayette Park

    Another neighborhood park we frequent when walking in Pacific Heights.

  • Larkmead

    If you’re planning a trip to wine country, this is the winery you shouldn’t miss.  It’s our favorite by a wide margin.  We’ve visited perhaps a dozen times since Mike and Katie introduced us and have gotten lots of others hooked since then.  Call 707-942-0167 to make a reservation.

  • Little Star Pizza

    This is San Francisco’s signature pizza: a cornmeal crusted version of Chicago deep-dish.  Rumored to be President Obama’s favorite.

  • Louie’s

    If you’re exploring the western part of the city, walk the Land’s End trail, climb around the ruins of the Sutro Baths, and definitely stop by this diner that’s been overlooking the Pacific Ocean since 1937.

  • Marin Headlands

    Directly across the Golden Gate bridge are the Marin Headlands where you can hike, bike, or visit Rodeo Beach.  The 18-mile loop that overlooks the city for a few miles is one of our favorite bike rides.

  • Muir Woods

    Hiking amongst the redwoods is tough to beat.  The park is very close to the city but parking is rough on weekends.

  • Powell Street Cable Car Turnaround

    The southern end of the cable car is in Union Square, home to basically all the shopping in San Francisco.  The Hyde Street line takes you through Russian Hill (our neighborhood) on the way to Lombard Street and Fisherman’s Wharf.

  • Rose’s

    More brunch!  Cute, French-style place with good coffee.  At the western end of the busy part of Union Street in the Marina.

  • Swensen’s

    Old-timey ice cream shop along the Hyde Street cable car.  Just steps from our apartment.  Katherine prefers toasted almond.  Richard favors chocolate chip cookie dough.

  • Presidio Social Club

    It’s only appropriate for our reception to be in a restaurant.  The Presidio Social Club was at one time a barracks and now serves brunch and dinner in one of the most beautiful places in San Francisco.

    Ceremony and reception

    Please arrive by 11:30am for the ceremony.  The reception will immediately follow with lunch, drinks, and dancing.

    Directions

    The Presidio is located at the northern tip of San Francisco.  The Presidio Social Club is just inside the Lombard Street entrance.  Direct your cab driver to Lombard and Lyon (pronounced like the animal), enter the Presidio through the Lombard Gate, and take an immediate left at Ruger Street.  The Presidio Social Club will be visible on your right.  Follow the map above or enter 563 Ruger Street into your GPS.

    Parking

    There is free parking in front of and behind the restaurant and along the tennis courts nearby.  If that fills up, continue west on Lombard, turn right on Presidio Boulevard, and try the first two lots you come across.  Or, if you’re up for a challenge, you can try your parking luck in the neighborhood along Lyon Street.

  • The Stanford Court

    The official hotel of this wedding, the Stanford Court is on Nob Hill at the intersection of two cable car lines and within sometimes steep walking distance of the bay, Russian Hill, Union Square, and Fisherman’s Wharf.

    Reserve your room for the nights of the 27th and 28th online or call 415-989-3500 and mention the Crowley/Clapham wedding to reserve longer blocks between the 24th and 31st at the same rate.  The group rate is $179 per night.  More about the hotel »
